# Commitment Policy

## 1. Purpose
This policy outlines the scheduling expectations, minimum time commitments, and reporting requirements for freelance contractors engaged with **K'fe Meetup**. It is designed to ensure consistent progress and collaboration while maintaining the flexibility inherent in a freelance arrangement.

## 2. Scope
This policy applies to all freelance contractors and independent professionals providing services to **K'fe Meetup**.

## 3. Work Hours and Minimum Commitment
- **Flexible Schedule:** As a freelance contractor, you have the flexibility to determine your own working hours and days, provided you meet your project deliverables and the minimum weekly time commitment.
- **Minimum Weekly Requirement:** All contractors are required to complete and log a minimum of **2 hours of work per week**.
- **Work Week Definition:** The standard work week for tracking purposes begins on Monday and ends on Sunday at 11:59 PM [Time Zone].

## 4. Break Periods
Because contractors determine their own schedules, you are responsible for managing your own meal and rest breaks. Break times are unbilled and do not count toward the 2-hour minimum requirement.

## 5. Availability and Communication
- **Responsiveness:** While your specific working hours are flexible, contractors are expected to remain reasonably responsive to team communications (e.g., emails, messages) within [e.g., 24-48 hours].
- **Mandatory Weekly Meeting:** All contractors are required to attend a weekly team meeting every **Friday from 8:00 PM to 9:00 PM [Time Zone]**. The 1 hour spent in this meeting counts toward your minimum weekly requirement of 2 hours.
- **Other Meetings/Syncs:** Any additional requested team meetings or syncs will be scheduled in advance at a mutually agreed-upon time.

## 6. Project Management and Version Control (GitHub)
To ensure transparency, collaboration, and a streamlined workflow, **K'fe Meetup** exclusively uses **GitHub** for all project-related activities:
- **Version Control Workflow:** We utilize a strict **Fork-based workflow** rather than a shared-branching model. Contractors must fork the official K'fe Meetup repository to their personal GitHub accounts, commit changes within their fork, and submit Pull Requests from their fork to the main upstream repository. Direct branching or pushing to the main K'fe Meetup repositories is not permitted.
- **Project Management:** All task tracking, issue reporting, and task assignments will be managed through **GitHub Projects**. Contractors are expected to update the status of their assigned issues as they work.
- **Roadmap & Documentation:** The project roadmap, milestones, and technical documentation will be maintained within GitHub. It serves as the single source of truth for the project's direction.


## 7. Logging and Reporting Hours
- **Time Tracking:** Contractors are expected to accurately track their time spent on K'fe Meetup tasks.
- **Submission Deadline:** A log of hours worked must be submitted via [Time Tracking Software/Email/Spreadsheet] by [e.g., Monday at 10:00 AM] for the previous week to ensure the 2-hour minimum has been met and to process invoices.

## 8. Planned and Unplanned Unavailability
- **Planned Time Off:** If you plan to be completely unavailable for a full week or more and will be unable to meet the 2-hour minimum, you must notify your K'fe Meetup point of contact at least **[e.g., 1 week]** in advance.
- **Emergencies/Missed Meetings:** In the event of an unexpected emergency or illness that prevents you from completing the minimum weekly hours or attending the mandatory Friday meeting, please notify us as soon as practically possible. 

## 9. Non-Compliance
Consistently failing to meet the minimum 2-hour weekly requirement, failing to use GitHub for project workflows, or missing mandatory meetings without prior communication disrupts project momentum. Such failure may result in a review of the working relationship and could lead to the termination of the freelance agreement.
